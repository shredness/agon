Agon — User guide

Agon tracks Relative Density (RD) - your total session volume divided by total time multiplied by bodyweight. A rising RD means you are getting stronger relative to your bodyweight, regardless of whether that weight is going up or down.
Workout

Log each training session here. Set the date, your bodyweight, and optional session notes. Add exercises one by one - each set gets its own load, reps, and time. The app calculates volume, density, and RD automatically as you build the session. Hit Save to commit. Saved sessions appear in History.

  • Bar type - toggle between Standard (total weight) and Vitruvian (weight per side x2)
  • Bodyweight exercises - choose Normal, Vest (adds lbs), or Assisted (subtracts %)
  • Crown icon 👑 in History - flags when an exercise hit its rep trigger (50 reps at same load), signaling time to increase weight
History

Filterable log of all sessions. Defaults to the current month. Use the date range and exercise filter to look further back. Edit or delete sessions from here. The delta badge shows RD change vs the prior session.

Trend

Weekly RD and bodyweight overlaid on a dual-axis chart. Colored bands show your training phases (Weight Loss, Cut, Recomp, Bulk, etc). The Recomp score card tells you whether you are building strength while losing weight, maintaining, or regressing.

  • Training phases - add phases to track your training cycles. Phases inform the AI and the macro calculator
  • Macro calculator - computes BMR, TDEE, and daily macro targets based on your profile and current phase. The "Build a meal plan with AI" button pre-fills the Insights tab with a prompt based on your macros
  • Macro targets by phase - retroactive macro history across all logged phases using average bodyweight per phase period
Progress

Per-exercise analysis. Select any logged exercise to see top-set load over time, estimated 1RM (Epley formula), PR table by rep count, and 4-week volume trend. The 7-day volume and Strength vs Peak cards show how the selected exercise is tracking. The Strength Maintenance table at the bottom shows current vs peak load for every exercise with a color-coded ratio.

Insights

AI-powered analysis of your training data. Ask plain-English questions and get answers grounded in your actual session history, profile, active phase, and protocols. Requires a Gemini or Claude API key set in Settings. Conversation persists across sessions.

  • Example questions: "Am I losing strength on my pulls?", "Which sessions had the best RD?", "How does my RD compare between my Weight Loss and Recomp phases?"
  • The "Build a meal plan with AI" button on the Trend tab sends your macro targets directly here
Exercises

The shared exercise bank. Add, edit, or remove exercises. Each exercise has a tool type (Bar, Cable, Dumbbell, Kettlebell, Machine, Bodyweight), a multiplier that determines how load is calculated, muscle group tags, and a load hint. Changes apply to all users.

Data

Import and export your session data as Excel files.

  • Import - download the template, fill it out (each row is one set), upload it. The Phase column is optional but will create phase records automatically from consecutive same-phase dates
  • Export - filter by date range (last 30/90/180/365 days or custom) and by phase before downloading. Exports a full Log sheet (all sets with Phase column), a Trend sheet, and an ExerciseBank sheet
Settings (click your username)

Opens via clicking your username in the top-right corner.

  • Profile - name, DOB, gender, height, target bodyweight, training week start, activity level. Used by the macro calculator and AI Insights
  • Protocols - supplements, peptides, and medications. Passed to the AI as context only. Agon does not provide medical advice
  • Account - change your password
  • External API - generate a read-only API key for your data. Share the endpoint URL with any external AI or tool to give it access to your full training history. Revoke at any time
  • AI Insights - connect a Gemini or Claude API key. Keys are encrypted and stored on your server

strength through loss
Agon tracks your Relative Density - a single number that tells you how much work you can do per minute relative to your bodyweight.
As you lose weight, RD tells you whether you are also losing strength - or outpacing it. It works just as well for bulk and cut cycles, recomps, and peak phases.
Built for athletes who care about relative strength, not just numbers on a bar.

How Agon works

Workout

Log every session here. Each set gets a load, reps, and time. RD is calculated automatically. The 👑 crown in History flags when an exercise hits its rep trigger - time to add weight.

Trend + Phases

Weekly RD and bodyweight on one chart, with colored phase bands. Add training phases (Weight Loss, Recomp, Bulk, etc) to annotate your history. The macro calculator uses your phase and profile to give you daily calorie and macro targets.

Ask plain-English questions about your training data. Powered by Gemini or Claude - add an API key in Settings (click your username). Questions like "Am I losing strength on my pulls?" get answers grounded in your actual sessions.

Data

Import historical sessions from Excel using the provided template. Export with optional date range and phase filters. Generate a read-only External API key in Settings to share your data with any AI or external tool.
